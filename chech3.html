Ch3-Atoms And Molecule

Introduction

Antoine L. Lavoisier laid the foundation of chemical sciences by establishing two important laws of chemical combination.

Laws of chemical combination

the following two laws of chemical combination were proposed by Lavoisier and Joseph Proust.

Law of conservation of mass

Mass can neither be created nor destroyed in a chemical reaction.

Law of constant proportions

In a chemical substance the elements are always present in definte proportions by mass.

Dalton's atomic theory

The main postulates of Dalton's atomic theory are as follows:

  • All matter is made up of very tiny particles called atoms, which participate in chemical reactions.
  • Atoms are indivisible particles, which can't be created or destroyed in a chemical reaction.
  • Atoms of a given element are identical in mass and chemical properties.
  • Atoms of different elements have different masses and chemical properties.
  • Atoms combine in the ratio of small whole numbers to form Compounds.
  • The relative number and kinds of atoms are constant in a given compound.

How symbols of elements are formed?

Symbols of elements are formed by using the first letter or the first two letters of their names. Examples: Chlorine- Cl, Zinc- Zn, etc. Other symbols have been taken from the names of elements in latin,greek or german. Examples:The symbol of iron is Fe from its latin name ferrum, sodium is Na from natrium, potassium is K from kalium.

Atomic mass

Atomic mass is the total mass of an atom, measured in atomic mass units (u). One atomic mass unit is defined as 1/12th of the mass of a carbon-12 atom.

What is a molecule?

A molecule is defined as the smallest particle of an element or compound that can exist independently and retain its chemical properties.

Molecules of elements

The molecules of an element are constituted of two or more atoms of the same element. Molecules of many elements, such as argon(Ar), helium(He), etc., are made up of only one atom of that element. The number of atoms constituting a molecule is known as its atomicity.

atomicity of some elements:

  • Argon (Ar)- Monoatomic
  • Helium (He)- Monoatomic
  • Oxygen (O₂)- Diatomic
  • Hydrogen (H₂)- Diatomic
  • Nitrogen (N₂)- Diatomic
  • Chlorine (Cl₂)- Diatomic
  • Phosphorus (P₄)- Tetraatomic
  • Sulphur (S₈)- Poly-atomic

Molecules of compounds

The molecules of a compound are constituted of two or more atoms of different elements.

What is ion?

Compounds composed of metals and non-metals contain charged species. the charged species are known as ions.
  • Positively charged ions are called cations.
  • Negatively charged ions are called anions.
A group of atoms carrying a charge is called a polyatomic ion.

Writing chemical formulae

The rules for writing chemical formulae are as follows:
  • The valenies or charge on the ion must balance.
  • When a compound is formed between a metal and a non-metal, the symbol of the metal is written first followed by the symbol of the non-metal.
  • In compounds containing polyatomic ions, the number of ions present in the compound is indicated by enclosing the formula of ion in a bracket(parentheses) and writing the number of ions outside the bracket. Example: Ca(NO₃)₂

Quick notes only! for the formation of chemical formulae, refer to your NCERT book.

The simplest compounds, which are made up of two different elements, are called binary compounds.

Molecular mass

Molecular mass is the sum of the atomic masses of all the atoms present in a molecule. Example: The molecular mass of water (H₂O) is calculated as follows:
  • Atomic mass of H = 1 u
    atomic mass of O = 16 u
    Molecular mass of H₂O = (2 X 1 u) + (1 X 16 u) = 18 u

Formula unit mass

Formula unit mass is the sum of the atomic masses of all the atoms present in a formula unit of a compound. The formula unit mass is calculated in the same manner as molecular mass. The only difference is that formula unit mass is used for ionic compounds.
